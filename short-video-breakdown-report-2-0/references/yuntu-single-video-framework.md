# 巨量云图单条视频内容资产框架

Use this reference when a 2.0 report needs detailed marketing diagnosis, tables, or a next-round content brief.

## 1. One-Line Judgment

Write one sentence that answers:

`这条内容影响了谁，用什么机制完成种草，是否值得放大，后链路应该接到哪里，下次复用什么。`

Example structure:

`这条视频更像[TA/场景]的[痛点教育/心智种草/成交转化/搜索激发]资产，核心强点是[hook/proof/scene/persona]，建议[优先加热/谨慎加热/只复用脚本/待核验]，后链路应承接到[搜索词/直播间/商品页/品专]。`

## 2. Content Asset Positioning

| Field | What To Fill | Evidence Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 品牌/产品 | Product and category | Use visible product info or user input |
| 营销目标 | 拉新, 种草, 搜索, 直播引流, 成交, 复投 | Infer from content only when explicit data is absent |
| 目标TA | Who should self-identify | Use spoken copy, subtitle labels, scenes, creator persona |
| 达人角色 | Expert, peer, lifestyle, reviewer, merchant, story actor | Verify from video behavior, not follower data unless supplied |
| 声线/音色角色 | tired mom, calm expert, urgent friend, excited reviewer, restrained authority, amused peer | Use audible delivery, not only transcript words |
| 链路角色 | 星, 推, 搜, 直, 复盘 | Pick primary and secondary roles |
| 证据等级 | 视频已验证, 表格/平台信号, 待核验 | Never mix evidence levels silently |

## 3. Creator Fit

Score and explain:

- `TA overlap`: does the creator/persona speak like the target user or trusted recommender?
- `vocal/emotional fit`: does the voice/timbre carry the right emotion for the scene, such as tiredness, urgency, intimacy, relief, expert calm, excitement, or crying edge?
- `content label`: does the creator's format make the product claim natural?
- `category credibility`: is there a reason viewers believe this person on this product?
- `scene credibility`: does the scene make the need feel real now?
- `risk`: mismatch, excessive hard sell, unclear identity, or weak relevance.

## 4. Hook Diagnosis 2.0

Keep the 1.0 hook analysis, then add two extra questions:

- `voice/timbre emotion`: what does the speaker's sound make the viewer feel before the words are fully processed?
- `TA filter`: does the opening immediately tell the right person this is for them?
- `handoff promise`: does the opening create a reason to search, click, enter live room, or continue watching?

Hook mechanisms to identify:

- language hook: conflict, pain, promise, curiosity, warning, comparison
- voice hook: fatigue, urgency, nasal tone, crying edge, whisper, laughter, calm authority, broadcast tone, dialect, speed, pause, breath, volume
- persona hook: identity, role, relationship, expert/peer signal
- action hook: entering, pulling, tearing, pouring, applying, close contact, reaction
- rhythm hook: BGM beat, subtitle pop, cut-point, zoom, object switch
- search hook: product name, problem word, function word, comparison word

## 5. Proof Chain

Analyze whether the video builds this chain:

`hook -> pain/scene -> product claim -> proof/sensory evidence -> trust cue -> search/conversion cue`

Common gaps:

- pain is strong but product solution is late
- ingredient/technology appears but viewer benefit is unclear
- taste/texture is claimed but not visually or aurally proven
- authority claim appears without relevance to the target user
- product is seeded but no search word or CTA is left
- CTA exists but the prior proof is not strong enough
- voice tone contradicts the scene, for example cheerful hard-sell voice over a tired-parent pain point, or broadcast tone where peer intimacy is needed

## 6. Viral And Heat Diagnosis

Do not label measured viral status unless platform data is supplied. Use `signal` and `potential`.

| Type | Creative Signals | Needed Data To Confirm |
|---|---|---|
| 量爆信号 | strong stop-scrolling hook, novelty, entertainment, high rhythm, comment trigger, shareable premise | natural playback percentile, completion, interaction |
| 质爆信号 | clear TA, strong product relevance, credible proof, strong benefit, search/进店 reason | A3新增率, A3新增量, search lift, 泛进店, conversion |
| 加热价值 | useful intent can be amplified, content has proof and handoff, audience is not too broad | star/heat spend, cost per A3/search/进店/conversion |

Recommendation labels:

- `优先加热`: strong creative evidence plus supplied or likely useful intent signals.
- `小预算试热`: creative mechanism is promising but proof/data incomplete.
- `只复用不加热`: good idea/structure but weak product intent or conversion handoff.
- `谨慎`: broad entertainment, weak product relevance, risky claim, or unclear TA.
- `待核验`: cannot decide without platform metrics or video access.

## 7. Search Handoff

Extract words the viewer may search after watching:

| Search Type | Examples | Content Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 品牌词 | brand name, nickname | logo, spoken brand, package close-up |
| 产品词 | SKU, series, category shorthand | visible product name |
| 功效词 | benefit, problem solution | claim/subtitle |
| 场景词 | use occasion, user situation | scene/story |
| 对比词 | vs competitor, alternative, ranking | comparison or objection |
| 小蓝词 | short exact phrase to pin/guide | memorable phrase or product nickname |

If no search cue exists, recommend the smallest fix: add spoken product name, subtitle keyword, comment pin, package close-up, or search phrase near CTA.

## 8. Conversion Handoff

Choose the likely next stop:

- `直播间`: if urgency, demo, offer, Q&A, or repeated explanation is needed.
- `商品页`: if the product and benefit are already clear.
- `品牌搜索/品专`: if the video creates curiosity but needs more official proof.
- `门店/本地页`: if location, appointment, or offline conversion matters.
- `私信/表单`: if consultative sales or lead collection matters.
- `下一条内容`: if proof is insufficient and the viewer needs education first.

For each real beat, include only rows that matter:

| Row | Purpose |

| 搜索词/承接 | likely search or next step |
| 强点 | what works |
| 风险 | missing proof, unclear TA, weak CTA, compliance risk |

Table emphasis rules:

- Use `<strong class="key-blue">...</strong>` for search words, handoff paths, CTA,加热策略, and next actions.
- Use `<strong class="key-green">...</strong>` for strong TA fit, strong proof, reusable patterns, and positive signals.
- Use `<strong class="key-dark">...</strong>` for important neutral labels such as product names, persona labels, or core scene nouns.
- Highlight words or short clauses only. Do not color entire paragraphs or every metric; the emphasis should guide scanning, not decorate the table.
